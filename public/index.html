free resources

POSTED ON: tuesday, dec 1st, 2020

Here are a few of my favorite resources to use when designing for the web!

  • w3schools.com is an incredibly trustworthy source. They have tutorials and easy-to-understand examples for ALL aspects of web development.

  • pexels.com is the absolute BEST free stock photos.
    (I have my own photos up on this site, too!)

  • iconmonstr.com & undraw.co, free icons! free svg illustrations! use them anywhere!

  • leonardocolor.io it's like Adobe Color, but way more in-depth. I love using this site whenever I need colors to design something that really needs to be accessible and easy to understand.

  • codepen.io has templates & examples that come in handy, especially for interactive elements. If you click on the sections under Portfolio to the right, that drop-down slider (called an accordion) is something you can find on codepen.
This is the shortest list I could come up with. To be honest, there is an endless amount of resources out there for all types of designers and developers. Working with code is fun, exciting, frustrating and gratifying. Even if you don't think you'll need it for your career, it is a great skill to have, and a free hobby to learn.

I've been using HTML & CSS since I was a literal child in 2005, and the internet has changed a LOT since then. But, I still remember so much of what I picked up on my own over the years, and I've been able to build upon this skillset with very little effort. I am by no means a professional- I've only taken one semester of a formal web development class. My point is that knowing this stuff can only ever help you. And you never know, you might love it!
